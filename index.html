Leadership Camden Military Academy

Turning Around a Platoon by Reading What Rank Couldn't See

At Camden Military Academy I was promoted to Platoon Leader — 2nd Lieutenant, responsible for 25 cadets who were genuinely difficult to manage. The easy move was authority. I had the rank to use it. I didn't.

Problem

25 cadets, high friction, low cohesion. Standard discipline hadn't worked. The rank gave me authority but authority alone wasn't moving anything.

Approach

One-on-one conversations with every cadet — not to deliver orders but to find the actual root of what was going on. Some needed structure. Some needed to feel seen. Some had things beneath the surface that explained everything.

What I built

Trust. One person at a time. A platoon where people knew I had read their situation accurately — and that I'd use that to help them, not manage them.

Result

By end of semester, the platoon had turned around completely. We won the award for best platoon.

Finance & Operations 2023–2024

Turning Around a Chapter $30K in Debt

As Exchequer of Alpha Epsilon Pi, I inherited a chapter deep in debt with the national organization. Collecting dues from brothers who couldn't or wouldn't pay was the core problem. I didn't treat every conversation the same.

Problem

$30,000+ in debt, no financial infrastructure, and a dues collection problem that defaulted to confrontation.

Approach

Read each person's situation individually — who needed a payment plan, who needed accountability, who needed me to back off and return differently. Negotiated debt forgiveness terms with the national office in parallel.

What I built

A full financial system: formalized board structures, systematic accounting, and accounts receivable tracking. The relationships held throughout.

Result

Resolved $30K in debt. Generated a 300% increase in chapter funding. The controls outlasted my tenure.

Entrepreneurship 2022–2025

Building a Business Entirely on Trust

Founded Elite Academic Coaching from scratch — no paid marketing, no agency, no cold outreach. Grew entirely through referrals by delivering consistent results and building real relationships with families.

Problem

Students needed reliable academic support. The existing market was expensive agencies with inconsistent quality and zero personal accountability.

Approach

Let results do the outreach. Kept clients for an average of 2+ years. Built a reporting loop with parents — email summaries after every session — so trust compounded over time.

What I built

A remote operation with 7+ tutors, accurate P&L forecasting, and predictable recurring cash flow — bootstrapped, no outside capital.

Result

3 years of sustained profitability. 100% referral-driven. Zero paid acquisition.
