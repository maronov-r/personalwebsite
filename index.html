Finance & Operations 2023–2024

Restructuring Alpha Epsilon Pi's Finances from the Ground Up

When I took over as Exchequer of my fraternity chapter, it was carrying over $30,000 in debt with no clear financial infrastructure. I treated it like a turnaround — negotiated with the national office, redesigned the dues system, and built controls that made the chapter financially self-sustaining.

Problem

The chapter had accumulated $30,000+ in debt and lacked any formal financial system — no structured dues collection, no board accountability, no forecasting.

Approach

Negotiated debt forgiveness terms directly with the national office, then rebuilt the revenue side: restructured dues with payment incentives and flexible plans to maximize collection rates.

What I built

A full financial infrastructure — formalized board meeting structures, systematic accounting processes, and accounts receivable tracking to maintain clarity and accountability.

Result

Resolved $30,000+ in debt. Generated a 300% increase in chapter funding. Established durable financial controls that outlasted my tenure.
